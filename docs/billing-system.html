Credit Valuation Rationale

Why $0.05 per credit?

Simple mental model

Pro user pays $99 for 2,000 credits. That's $0.0495/credit — close enough to $0.05 to be intuitive. A typical article costs ~16 credits = ~$0.80. Users can reason: "an article costs under a dollar."

Why 5 credits per 1K words?

25x markup on Gemini Flash

Gemini Flash text costs ~$0.004/article. We charge 5 credits ($0.25) for text alone. The 25x markup provides cushion for fallback models (GPT-4o costs ~$0.03), retries, and infrastructure.

Why 2 credits for Flux images?

8.3x markup covers storage + bandwidth

Flux 2 Dev costs $0.012/image. 2 credits = $0.10 (8.3x markup). Covers: failed generations needing retry, R2 storage costs for hosting images, bandwidth for serving them, and general overhead.

Why not per-token billing?

Predictability wins

Token counts are opaque to users, small prompt variations cause fluctuating bills, and images dominate costs anyway (not tokens). Credits give a simpler, more predictable experience that users can plan around.
